# KRAS G12C Clinical Trial Landscape — 2025-2026

## Overview

KRAS G12C is a validated oncogenic driver mutation occurring in ~13% of NSCLC, ~3-4% of CRC, and lower frequencies in other solid tumors. The field has evolved from proof-of-concept (CodeBreaK 100) to a crowded Phase 3 competitive landscape with multiple inhibitors and combination strategies.

**First FDA approvals:**
- Sotorasib (LUMAKRAS) — May 2021 (NSCLC), August 2022 (CRC + panitumumab)
- Adagrasib (KRAZATI) — December 2022 (NSCLC), June 2024 (CRC + cetuximab)

---

## Approved KRAS G12C Inhibitors

### Sotorasib (Amgen — LUMAKRAS)

**Mechanism:** Irreversible covalent inhibitor binding to G12C cysteine, locks KRAS in inactive GDP-bound state. Does not affect wild-type KRAS.

**Indications:**
1. NSCLC — single agent, 2nd-line+ after prior systemic therapy
2. mCRC — in combination with panitumumab, 3rd-line+ after fluoropyrimidine/oxaliplatin/irinotecan

**Dosing:** 960 mg orally once daily (NSCLC); 960 mg daily + panitumumab 6 mg/kg IV q2w (CRC)

**Key Adverse Reactions (NSCLC):**
- Diarrhea (42%), musculoskeletal pain (35%), nausea (26%), fatigue (26%)
- Hepatotoxicity (25%, Grade 3-4 in 12%)
- ILD/pneumonitis (0.8% fatal)

**Key Adverse Reactions (CRC + panitumumab):**
- Rash (87%), dry skin (28%), diarrhea (28%), stomatitis (26%), fatigue (21%)
- Rash Grade 3-4: 26%

**Clinical Trials (18 in oncology-db):**
- CodeBreaK 200 — Phase 3 (sotorasib vs. docetaxel in 2nd-line NSCLC)
- CodeBreaK 300 — Phase 3 (sotorasib + panitumumab vs. SOC in CRC)
- CodeBreaK 202 — Phase 3 (sotorasib + platinum doublet vs. pembrolizumab + chemo in 1st-line)
- Multiple Phase 1/2 combinations including trastuzumab deruxtecan, carfilzomib, ladarixin

---

### Adagrasib (Mirati/BMS — KRAZATI)

**Mechanism:** Irreversible KRAS G12C inhibitor, similar covalent binding mechanism to sotorasib.

**Indications:**
1. NSCLC — single agent, 2nd-line+ after prior systemic therapy
2. mCRC — in combination with cetuximab, 3rd-line+ after fluoropyrimidine/oxaliplatin/irinotecan

**Dosing:** 600 mg orally twice daily

**Key Adverse Reactions (NSCLC):**
- Nausea (69%), diarrhea (70%), vomiting (56%), fatigue (59%)
- Hepatotoxicity (37%, Grade 3-4 in 10%)
- QTc prolongation (20%, Grade 3-4 in 6%)
- Renal impairment (36%, Grade 3-4 in 6%)
- ILD/pneumonitis (2.6%)

**Key Adverse Reactions (CRC + cetuximab):**
- Rash (84%), nausea (68%), diarrhea (65%), vomiting (57%), fatigue (57%)
- Rash Grade 3-4: 4.3%

**Clinical Trials (10 in oncology-db):**
- KRYSTAL-7 — Phase 2/3 (adagrasib +/- pembrolizumab +/- chemo in 1st-line NSCLC)
- KRYSTAL-4 — Phase 3 (adagrasib + pembrolizumab + chemo vs. placebo + pembrolizumab + chemo in 1st-line non-squamous NSCLC)
- KRYSTAL-1 — Phase 1/2 (multiple combinations including olaparib, SRS for brain mets)

## Resistance Mechanisms

### Primary (De novo) Resistance

- KRAS amplification
- Concurrent STK11/LKB1 loss (common co-mutation)
- KEAP1 mutation (NFE2L2 pathway dysregulation)
- Activation of bypass pathways (HER2, MET, EGFR)

### Acquired Resistance

**EGFR-mediated (particularly in CRC):**
- EGFR activation identified as key resistance mechanism in KRAS G12C CRC
- Rationale for combining KRAS G12C inhibitors with anti-EGFR antibodies (panitumumab, cetuximab)

**RAS pathway:**
- Secondary KRAS mutations (Y96C, Q99H, R68S)
- KRAS amplification as acquired resistance
- NRAS mutations

**Histologic transformation:**
- SCLC transformation (similar to EGFR TKI resistance)

## Clinical Trial Matching Considerations

### For NSCLC Patients

**1st-line (treatment-naive):**
- Phase 3 trials dominate: pembrolizumab combinations, osimertinib combinations being explored
- PD-L1 TPS >=50%: Consider MK-1084 (calderasib) + pembrolizumab trial
- Biomarker: KRAS G12C + PD-L1 expression required for most 1st-line trials

**2nd-line+ (post-platinum chemo):**
- Standard-of-care: sotorasib or adagrasib monotherapy
- Trials: D-1553 vs. docetaxel (Chinese-developed G12C inhibitor)
- Combination trials: KRAS G12C + immunotherapy rechallenge

**Brain metastases:**
- Adagrasib + SRS trial (NCT06248606) — focused on untreated brain mets
- Sotorasib + T-DXd trial (NCT07012031) — may have CNS activity

### For CRC Patients

- KRAS G12C CRC has limited options; sotorasib + panitumumab or adagrasib + cetuximab are approved in 3rd-line
- 1st-line trials: FOLFIRI + sotorasib + panitumumab (CodeBreaK 300 successor)
- BBO-10203 combination with BBO-8520 being explored

### Key Eligibility Biomarkers

| Biomarker | Test | Trial Relevance |
|-----------|------|----------------|
| KRAS G12C | NGS (tissue or liquid biopsy) | Required for all KRAS G12C trials |
| PD-L1 TPS | IHC (22C3 or SP263) | 1st-line NSCLC trials |
| STK11/LKB1 | NGS | May predict resistance to immunotherapy |
| KEAP1 mutation | NGS | May predict resistance to immunotherapy |
| HER2 amplification | FISH/IHC | Combined EGFR + HER2 trials |
